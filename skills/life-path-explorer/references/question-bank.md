# Question Bank

Complete question bank for the Life Path Explorer. Each phase contains 5 core questions with suggested follow-up probes. Questions should be asked one at a time, adapting order and follow-ups based on user responses.

---

## Phase 1: Values (价值观)

Values are behavioral principles and bottom lines. They determine what to pursue and what to reject.

### Q1: Admired People
**Ask:** "Is there someone you deeply respect or admire? It can be someone real or fictional. What specifically do you admire about them?"

**Follow-ups:**
- "Why does that quality matter to you?"
- "Do you see that quality in yourself?"
- "What would it look like to live by that value?"

### Q2: Defining Moments
**Ask:** "Think back to your teenage years or the most active period of your life — was there an event that deeply shaped who you are today?"

**Follow-ups:**
- "What did that event change in you?"
- "What did you start believing — or stop believing — because of it?"
- "If you could tell your younger self one thing about that experience, what would it be?"

### Q3: What Needs Fixing
**Ask:** "What about today's society or your surrounding environment feels wrong to you? What would you want to change?"

**Follow-ups:**
- "If you had unlimited power to change one thing, what would it be?"
- "Why does this bother you more than it bothers most people?"
- "Is there a personal experience behind this frustration?"

### Q4: Others' Perception
**Ask:** "If you asked your closest friends or family 'What kind of person am I? What do I value most?' — what do you think they'd say?"

**Follow-ups:**
- "Do you agree with their perception?"
- "Is there something you deeply value that others don't know about?"
- "Is there a gap between how others see you and how you see yourself?"

### Q5: Advice Given
**Ask:** "What advice do you most often give to others? Or flip it — what advice do you hate receiving from others?"

**Follow-ups:**
- "Why do you give that specific advice?"
- "The advice we give others often reveals our deepest beliefs. Does that resonate?"
- "What advice would you give your 20-year-old self?"

### Phase 1 Synthesis
After all questions, extract 3-5 value keywords. Present them as:
- **[Keyword]**: [One-sentence explanation based on user's own words]

Confirm with user: "These are the core values I'm seeing. Do they feel right? Anything to add or change?"

## Phase 2: Talent / Innate Ability (才能/天赋)

Talent is what comes naturally — things done effortlessly that others find difficult. Distinguish from learned skills.

### Q1: Peak Experience
**Ask:** "What was the most fulfilling, smoothest period of your life? What were you doing during that time?"

**Follow-ups:**
- "What was your daily state like during that period?"
- "Why did it feel so smooth — what specifically was working?"
- "What ability of yours was being fully utilized?"

### Q2: Impatience Signal (Critical Question)
**Ask:** "Think of a recent time you felt impatient or frustrated with someone — something you thought 'How is this hard? Isn't it obvious?' but they genuinely struggled with it."

**Follow-ups:**
- "This is a crucial signal — what feels 'obvious' to you but is genuinely difficult for others is likely your innate talent."
- "Can you think of more examples of this pattern?"
- "Have you been told 'How did you do that so easily?'"

### Q3: Others' Assessment
**Ask:** "If you asked people around you 'What am I good at?' — what would they say? And has any answer ever surprised you?"

**Follow-ups:**
- "Which answers surprised you? 'That counts as a skill?'"
- "Is there something you're good at that you've been taking for granted?"
- "What do people come to you for help with?"

### Q4: Fantasy Career
**Ask:** "If money and reality were completely irrelevant — you could do absolutely anything — what would you do?"

**Follow-ups:**
- "What about that attracts you — the content itself, or the lifestyle/way of working?"
- "Is there a common thread between that fantasy and your current situation?"
- "What's stopping you from moving toward that direction, even partially?"

### Q5: Past Wins
**Ask:** "What have you successfully accomplished so far? Even small things count — anything you made happen."

**Follow-ups:**
- "What ability did you use to make that happen — persistence, intuition, creativity, persuasion?"
- "Is there a pattern across your wins?"
- "What role do you naturally take in group projects or team settings?"

### Shadow Flip Reference Table

When users mention "weaknesses," consult this table:

| Surface Flaw | Hidden Talent |
|-------------|---------------|
| Slow / deliberate | Deep focus, thoroughness |
| Scattered / distracted | Fast associative thinking, high novelty detection |
| Too sensitive | High empathy, perceptiveness, emotional intelligence |
| Careless / sloppy | Brain prioritizes big-picture structural thinking |
| Talks too much / interrupts | Fast mental modeling, verbal processing speed |
| Indecisive | Sees multiple perspectives simultaneously |
| Stubborn | Strong conviction, persistence |
| Impatient | High standards, fast processing |
| Perfectionist | Quality orientation, attention to craft |
| "Lazy" | Energy conservation — selective engagement with high-value tasks |
| Daydreamer | Rich internal world, strong imagination and scenario-building |
| Argumentative | Analytical thinking, pattern detection |
| Restless | Needs intellectual stimulation, high cognitive drive |
| "Too idealistic" | Vision-driven, refuses to settle |

### Phase 2 Synthesis
Extract 3-5 core talents as verb phrases:
- **[Verb phrase]**: [Evidence from user's answers]

Confirm with user before proceeding.

## Phase 3: Passion / Interest (热情/梦想)

Passion must be pure interest — temporarily remove all "useful" and "profitable" filters.

### Q1: Voluntary Investment
**Ask:** "What have you willingly spent your own money on to learn or explore — not for career advancement, purely out of curiosity?"

**Follow-ups:**
- "What courses, books, or tools have you bought purely out of interest?"
- "If someone gave you a year off with full salary, what would you spend time learning?"
- "What YouTube channels, podcasts, or blogs do you follow for fun?"

### Q2: Content Landscape
**Ask:** "What's on your bookshelf, in your browser bookmarks, or in your watch history? What types of content dominate?"

**Follow-ups:**
- "If you started a blog or channel, what would you talk about?"
- "What topic can you talk about for hours without getting bored?"
- "What rabbit holes have you fallen into recently?"

### Q3: Personal Salvation
**Ask:** "Has there been a piece of knowledge, a book, a concept, or a field that 'saved' you during a difficult time?"

**Follow-ups:**
- "Why was it able to help you? What did you gain from it?"
- "Do you feel drawn to share that with others who might be going through similar things?"
- "Is this still an active interest?"

### Q4: Gratitude Signal
**Ask:** "Is there a person or experience you feel deeply grateful for? What made it meaningful?"

**Follow-ups:**
- "Gratitude often points to what we truly value. What does this tell you about yourself?"
- "Would you want to create that kind of experience for others?"

### Q5: Righteous Anger
**Ask:** "What about the world makes you angry or frustrated? If you could change one thing about how the world works, what would it be?"

**Follow-ups:**
- "The desire to change the world often hides enormous passion. Does this connect to anything you'd want to work on?"
- "Is this anger connected to a personal experience?"
- "If you had a platform with millions of followers, what message would you broadcast?"

### Phase 3 Synthesis
Extract 2-3 passion domains:
- **[Domain]**: [Why this area resonates, based on user's answers]

Confirm with user before proceeding to cross-matching.

## Cross-Matching Guide

### Step 1: Talent × Passion Matrix
Create combinations of each talent with each passion domain. For each combination, generate a concrete career/life direction.

Example:
- Talent: "Rapidly model complex systems" × Passion: "AI/Technology" → AI product strategy, technical architecture, AI startup founder
- Talent: "Persuade and reframe perspectives" × Passion: "Education" → Curriculum designer, educational content creator, coaching

### Step 2: Values Filter
For each generated direction, check against values:
- Does this direction allow living by the identified values?
- Which directions create value conflicts?
- Which directions maximize value alignment?

### Step 3: Present Top 2-3 Directions
For each direction, explain:
1. Why it fits (talent + passion evidence)
2. Values alignment assessment
3. A concrete small experiment to test it
